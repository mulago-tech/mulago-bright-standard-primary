Home

Welcome to Mulago Bright Standard Primary School — a beacon of academic excellence and moral upbringing since 1998. Founded by visionary educators Mr. Mukose and Ester Mukose, our school has nurtured thousands of children in the heart of Mulago.

🚨 URGENT: SCHOOL LAND INVASION & CLASSROOM DEMOLITION

Published: Last Week

In a horrifying and unlawful act of violence against education, armed individuals wielding sledgehammers and crowbars — reportedly hired by a powerful local businessman with alleged ties to high-ranking government officials — invaded Mulago Bright Standard Primary School in broad daylight last week. Their target? Our children’s classroom.

Over the course of a single night, they demolished an entire classroom block housing our Primary 3 and Primary 4 pupils — reducing desks, books, and dreams to rubble. This was not vandalism. This was a calculated land grab, disguised as development, aimed at stealing the future of Uganda’s most vulnerable: our children.

Founder Mr. Mukose, who resides on the school premises, bravely attempted to intervene and repeatedly called the police for help. Shockingly, officers refused to respond, citing “orders from above.” The school community — teachers, parents, and students — stood by helplessly, watching their sanctuary of learning destroyed.

The following day, Mr. Mukose was summoned by the Resident City Commissioner (RCC), who immediately ordered a halt to all demolition activities pending a full investigation. The RCC condemned the attack as “an assault on education and the future of our children” — a statement we welcome, but one that came too late to save our classrooms.

This is not the first time Mr. Mukose has faced intimidation. He has previously been arrested and imprisoned for whole six months under dubious charges — clearly an effort to silence him. He now asks: Why did no one act when we cried for help? Why must justice only knock after destruction is complete?

This is not just about bricks and mortar. It is about the right of every Ugandan child to learn in safety. It is about the rule of law. It is about whether wealth and connections can trample over the dreams of the innocent.
